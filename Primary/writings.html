So, you want to travel back

Published: April 19, 2025

"So, you want to travel back,
for you could not contain,
could not refrain,
that boundless hunger deep within,
For all the changes you could pinch,

So, you want to travel back,
to gain control that with time lost,
for all mistakes that bore a heavy cost,
reassure the freedom thereafter,
stripping off all thy past disasters,

So, you want to travel back,
For all the changes you could etch,
for all harmony that would cling,
from Serenity you longed to fetch,
from the compassion that you'd bring,

Like a hawk that couldn’t be caged anymore

Like tending a wound that couldn’t be restored

Like gazing at beauty that can’t be mirrored


And, So, you will travel back,
to arrive as peaceful as still marine water,
to garner stillness, that of a weaver,
whose final thread is laid,
with all the glory and praises,
that reality relentlessly raises,

So, you will travel back,
For the cosmic loom to set itself,
linear as it ever was,
for thy doomed fate to let itself,
bound as tight as ever was,

So, you will travel back,
never truly to embrace,
to forever unknowingly interlace,
the desperate want to travel back"

The Cost of Belonging

Published: April 18, 2025

Was I born different?
What did I do different that made me the way I am?

These are some of the questions that rebels like myself ask themselves at some point in life, probably after realizing how fucked up the world really is and how much control you really have, can’t put an age up at this, it’s different for everyone.

This might just also be the time that we find ourselves a bit more depressed than usual and start looking out for those tiny moments of happiness since life isn’t playdough anymore. We might find ourselves having thoughts of travelling back through time to when things were simpler, when we were not as rebellious as we are right now.

Let’s define our terms, shall we? What do I mean by a ‘rebel’ and being ‘rebellious’?

See I like to believe that the world is divided into two kinds of people, the usual sheep-like ‘Conformists’ and the ever changing, fed up with the system, ‘Rebels’

Why Are We Raised to Obey, Not to Think?

Why is it that as kids—and even as teens—we’re never really taught how to think? We're only taught how to obey. Respect your elders. Follow the rules. Conformity is the default setting, especially enforced by family and state. Parents are given a blank slate the moment we’re born. They can raise a rebel… or shape a command-obeying conformist.

So, here’s the question:
What’s right or wrong with either of these ideologies?
And the deeper one—how long can someone truly stay in a conformist state before cracking?

These questions unravel more beneath them. Which idea of liberation deserves more value? How much influence do parents really hold over the wiring of a child’s identity?

Let’s address the first question. Anyone with a functioning brain, basic observational skills, and even a hint of logic should already know the answer—given the world we live in today.

Let me be blunt: I’m heavily biased. I’m a free soul. I despise authority of any kind. I believe a man of logic should follow reasoning, not orders. And no, this isn’t some utopian delusion. We don't live in an ideal world. Anyone who's grown out of their childhood innocence can see it. It doesn't stop with just your own country—look around, dig deeper. You'll arrive at the same conclusion:
Everyone. Everywhere. Is messed up.

Society, globally, is broken. And here’s the part that stings: the majority of it is made up of conformists.

Look at Trump’s re-election. Look at a Chai-wala’s turned supreme leader riding the wave of blind devotion for over a decade. If that’s not enough to blame the downfall on mass conformity, I don’t know what is.

And rebels?
What do a handful of rebels even achieve—except martyrdom?

History has Proof. Narendra Dabholkar. Gauri Lankesh. Journalists. Whistleblowers. Silenced. Gunned down. Forgotten. Most conformists don’t even know about their sacrifices.

Some days, I find myself genuinely believing that this world mostly comprises of and run by retards.

Is it really that hard to not get brainwashed?
To hold an objective thought before buying into something?
To question anything you encounter?

Apparently, for most—it is.

And that’s terrifying.

Another question we don’t ask enough:
How much damage do cover-ups, lies, and secrecy cause when authorities mess up?
Maybe I’ll dig into that next time. But for now, let me say this:

Conformity—as a global disease—should be viewed with the same suspicion we reserve for communism (or any ideology when taken to the extreme). The world would be better off without blind followers.

And here comes the final—and maybe trickiest—question:
How long can one stay a conformist? And how much of that is because of parenting?

In my case, both my parents are conformists. The only rebellion they seem to show is towards me. They’ve never been the curious type. Never stirred trouble. Unless something extreme happens—or someone they care about is at risk—they’ll stay quiet. Safe. Predictable.

By that logic, I should’ve turned out exactly like them.

But I didn’t.

I’m the polar opposite. I question everything. I overthink everything.
Why? Probably because I surrounded myself with… no one.
No friends. Just books. YouTube. My own thoughts. Isolation breeds introspection.

This needs to be studied more, honestly. I can’t draw conclusions from just one case. But if I had to bet—I'd say rebels are born this way. I was fed the same ideas as everyone else. But something in me rejected them all.

And here I am.

Published: February 14, 2025

It's been so long that we have written anything and so much has happened and I don't mean to provide like a storytelling effect or something but really so much has happened. I discovered myself, I discovered that I was an asexual and personally I am disgusted by sex and anything related to that particular activity. This was a discovery that I made at around December, yeah, and I have only ever been more so confirmed of the fact that I am an asexual. So there's that and I didn't write anything particular during the interval that I was gone but I surely picked up some books which I've been reading and I'll share the image. I ordered them last month and they arrived last month only but I've begun reading them right now.

Sometimes I think I should start writing songs instead of writing poems. Maybe. We'll see. I am in the mood. I am starting to prepare for IPMAT And JIPMAT. I have been preparing, I'll say. Just because of the fact that I am surrounded by literal idiots. And I cannot endure any more of all this stupidity that I have endured so far. I need a break from all this. I need a better crowd around me. I need a better surrounding. That's why I have decided my past failure does not define who I am. And I'll be working hard to achieve this particular goal of mine. Because I only have like two months, to be frank with you. And this realization, although it came very late, I am glad that it did. And let's see what happens. I will try to write some blog posts or poems in between if I get the chance or the time to do so. Until then, I am not sure, but let's see.

There was this very fascinating, at least to me, a problem which I was stuck with up until now. This thought process of mine, which I'll summarize. Like all these past experiences which I unconsciously assume are of failure that plagued my mind, I don't know how to get over them or why haven't I internalized the fact that failure is just a part of the journey and not something to cling on to. What I realized over time or I realized like past few weeks is the fact that I am currently stuck in something totally stupid. I do realize that I don't like the crowd around me, yes. And the fact that I am clinging on to my so-called failures only to never try again is blatantly stupid. That is what I have been doing for the past few months, no not few months, for past an year or so. No one just gives up like that and I am only 19. This cannot be the way of life from now on. Like this cannot be my way of life for like ever in my life. This is just not me. I might be surrounded by idiots but I am not one. And just giving up on everything, passing your whole days doing worthless shit is just utterly idiotic and stupid. And if there is one thing I am totally sure of is that I am not stupid. I am smarter than the average and if so then I should act like one too.

I also realized one more thing. That depression, at least the one that I had, or my depression, is blatantly stupid. It was for me, at least. If you follow everything logically and just spend time with yourself and, you know, journal everything out like I did, and try to uncover the secrets of your thought process, I'll say, you might never get depressed. At least, this was the case with me.

Yesterday, out of nowhere, I just gave a mock of JIPMAT, and I scored around 110, which is surprising to be honest. I had zero to little preparation. I haven't even picked up a book related to academics in like 9 or 10 months, and I was able to score 110. I obviously scored the most in verbal ability and reading comprehension. That is one thing I'm pretty sure I'll need less preparation time for. The worst I did was in quant or mathematics, because obviously you need preparation for that. This is just fascinating to me, being able to score such marks without any preparation. That got me thinking of what would happen if I tried.


I tried to read all the books that arrived yesterday, and there is one book that hasn't arrived yet, that being 101 essays, that will change the way that you think. White Nights, I found a little confusing. The narration and the vocab isn't really friendly at all. God Delusion is pretty great, and I'm pretty sure that I'll be reading it cover to cover, and it's something that I'll live my life by. The most fun I've had was with Read People Like A Myth by Patrick King. It is pretty easy to understand, and I'm just a very big fan of human psychology, because you know, it helps me understand myself better, and the people around me even better. And I did learn many things halfway through chapter one, that I'm pretty sure I'll be writing a post on. So I guess I'll be looking forward to that.
